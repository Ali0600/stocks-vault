# Oracle FQ4: $638B AI backlog vs a debt-funded capex cycle (ORCL)

## Summary
An opinion column on [[ORCL]]'s Fiscal Q4 (FY ends May 31), which triggered a post-earnings
selloff. The routable substance — separate from the selloff and the price targets — is the
scale of Oracle's AI-cloud demand vs the cost to chase it: revenue $19.2B (+21%), OCI $5.8B
(+93%), a contracted backlog (RPO) that jumped to **$638B**, **$67B of AI contracts signed**
in the quarter, and a guided **FY27 capex (~$92.5B reported) that exceeds expected FY27
revenue** — funded by a planned **~$40B debt+equity raise** and pushing free cash flow
deeply negative. ORCL had no vault note, so I created a watchlist note under Software & Cloud
and wired NVDA→ORCL (OCI GPUs) into the supply chain. Source: Obsidian Web Clip; raw clip in
`_inbox/`. Price baseline is the 2026-06-12 close. Passing mentions: [[MSFT]], Amazon (AMZN),
Alphabet (GOOGL) — named only as better-capitalized hyperscaler peers, not routed.
