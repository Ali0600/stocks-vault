# Nebius Q1 revenue +684% and the run-rate ramp (NBIS)

## Summary
An opinion column on [[NBIS]] built around its Q1 2026 print: revenue grew **684%
year over year** as the company rapidly expanded its data-center footprint to meet AI
compute demand. The substance worth keeping is operational, not the opinion — sites
running ≥100 MW went from **1 (end-2025) to 7 (end-Q1 2026)**, and management guides
its annualized run-rate from **$1.25B → $1.9B → $7–9B** by end-2026. Publish date is a
Saturday, so the price baseline is the 2026-06-12 (Friday) close. Passing mentions:
[[NVDA]] (already an investor in and GPU supplier to Nebius — see its note) and [[INTC]]
(named only in an ad blurb), neither routed.

## Claims & Verification
- "Q1 (ended Mar 31) revenue growth was 684%" — **verified** · Q1 2026 revenue $399.0M
  vs Q1 2025 $50.9M = +684% (yfinance quarterly income statement; `revenueGrowth` 6.839)
- "≥100 MW data-center sites went from 1 at end-2025 to 7 by end-Q1 2026" —
  **company-reported** · operational disclosure, not independently checkable
- "Annualized run-rate $1.25B (end-2025) → $1.9B (end-Q1 2026), guided to $7B–$9B by
  end-2026" — **company-reported / guidance** · the $7–9B figure is forward guidance
- "Wall Street expects +551% revenue growth in 2026 and +224% in 2027" — **analyst
  forecast** · consensus estimate, unverified
- "Nvidia is invested in Nebius" — **previously verified** · the ~$2B NVIDIA investment
  is already on NBIS's Facts; not re-routed
- "Stock is up 135% in 2026" — **inaccurate** · actual 2026 YTD is +170% ($86.04 →
  $232.36, yfinance). A price-performance figure regardless, so **not routed** — the
  tracker app and the `## Snapshot` block cover price/returns.

## Takeaways Routed
- [[NBIS]] ← fact: Q1 2026 revenue $399.0M, +684% YoY (driven by data-center build-out,
  not M&A; verified). Fact: ≥100 MW sites 1→7 and run-rate $1.25B→$1.9B
  (company-reported). Speculation (open): run-rate guided to $7–9B by end-2026 + Street
  +551%/2026, +224%/2027. The +135% stock claim was fact-checked above (actually +170%)
  but **not routed** — price performance lives in the app + Snapshot.
