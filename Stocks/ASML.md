# ASML Holding N.V. (ASML)

## Overview
ASML makes the photolithography machines that print chip circuitry — and it is the sole maker of EUV (extreme-ultraviolet) scanners, the tools required for every leading-edge node. ~$718B market cap (~$1,860/share). The closest thing the whole semiconductor industry has to a single point of failure. Watchlist note: AI-supply-chain research, no position.

## What's Unique
A 100% monopoly on EUV lithography (~83% of the total lithography market) — Nikon and Canon abandoned EUV over a decade ago, leaving ASML alone at the most critical step of chipmaking.

### Only They Do
- The only company on Earth that makes EUV lithography scanners — no leading-edge chip ([[Foundry process nodes]]) can be printed without one; High-NA EUV (~$350M+ per system) extends the monopoly into the next nodes
- ~83% of all lithography; a ~€38.8B end-2025 backlog gives multi-year revenue visibility
- **Chokepoint:** Extreme — a literal sole-source monopoly on the single most essential tool in chipmaking; the deepest gate in the [[Wafer-fab equipment|WFE]] stack, arguably ahead of foundry itself, and geopolitically central (EUV is export-controlled away from China)

## Competitors
- [[AMAT]] · [[LRCX]] · [[KLAC]] — other WFE leaders (different steps; not lithography rivals)
- Nikon, Canon (external) — DUV-only; both exited EUV
- No EUV competitor exists

## Supply Chain
### Suppliers
