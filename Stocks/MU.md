## What's Unique
Memory is normally a commodity, but HBM — the stacked DRAM that sits on the GPU
package — has turned a slice of it into a supply-constrained oligopoly. Micron is
one of only three companies on Earth that can make HBM, and the only one
headquartered in the US.

### Only They Do
- One of only three HBM manufacturers worldwide (with SK Hynix and Samsung), and the only US-based memory maker — a near-impossible business to enter, given 18–36-month, multi-billion-dollar capacity cycles
- HBM3E qualified into [[NVDA]]'s accelerators; HBM4 validating through 2026; HBM capacity reported sold out through calendar 2026 at roughly an $8B annualized run-rate
- **Chokepoint:** High — AI accelerators can't run at full speed without HBM adjacent to the compute die, only three suppliers make it, and there is no spot market; Micron is #3 by share (~20%, behind SK Hynix's ~55–60%), so it is both part of a genuine bottleneck and the strategic US-based second source
