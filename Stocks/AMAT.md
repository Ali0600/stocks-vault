# Applied Materials, Inc. (AMAT)

## Overview
Applied Materials sells the equipment that makes chipmaking possible — deposition, etch, inspection — so it earns from everyone's fab expansion regardless of which chip designer wins. ~$395B market cap.

## What's Unique
The broadest wafer-fab-equipment portfolio spanning deposition, etch, and process diagnostics — so it captures spending across nearly every step of a fab build, independent of which chip designer or foundry ultimately wins.

### Only They Do
- Share leader in deposition (CVD/PVD/epitaxy) — the materials-engineering steps every leading-edge fab must buy, regardless of whose chips it makes
- Only equipment vendor spanning materials creation, modification, removal, and analysis in co-optimized "integrated materials solutions" — bundled tool flows for gate-all-around transistors and backside power that single-step rivals can't package

**Chokepoint:** High — five companies control essentially all wafer-fab equipment; every new fab (TSMC, Intel, Samsung) queues for its tools, and lead times stretch in upcycles. It shares overall WFE spend with Lam, KLA, Tokyo Electron, and ASML, so it gates *steps* (epitaxy, implant), not all of fab equipment.
