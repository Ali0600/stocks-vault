Story Highlights

- SoFi’s sharp pullback looks more tied to macro worries and investor sentiment than to any real breakdown in the business, which continues to show strong growth and improving profitability.
- With the company scaling its broader financial ecosystem and earnings expected to rise quickly, the current valuation may underestimate its longer-term upside.

SoFi [SOFI +3.32% ▲](https://www.tipranks.com/stocks/sofi) stock’s brutal sell-off could prove a golden opportunity, as its [recent weakness](https://www.tipranks.com/news/is-sofi-stock-a-buy-after-the-steep-ytd-selloff-wall-street-weighs-in) appears disconnected from the company’s excellent execution. There is indeed macroeconomic noise that can keep investors wary of the stock. Still, the digital bank is aggressively scaling its high-margin financial services ecosystem and is poised for explosive earnings growth in the coming years. The current valuation doesn’t really seem to price this potential in. For this reason, I am bullish on the SOFI stock.

## A Halved Share Price

SoFi has seen its share price get halved from its 52-week peak of $32.73. At around $17 today, it looks like the wheels have fallen off the cart entirely. Wall Street has thrown the stock into the penalty box, which I believe is the result of a mix of macro headwinds and shifting investor sentiment demanding immediate profitability.

![](https://blog.tipranks.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/06/SOFI-Stock-Analysis.jpg)

To some extent, we have to admit that the anxiety here is totally fair. We’re stuck with high rates for the foreseeable future, and it’s basically paralyzed the regenerative finance (ReFi) market. Nobody is touching student or personal loans with these kinds of numbers. Borrowers are just sitting out and hoping the macro environment turns around. On top of that, the fight for deposits is just ugly right now. SoFi has to offer crazy high annual percentage yields (APYs) just to keep cash from walking out the door. If they keep paying out more to depositors than they’re making on their own assets, that net interest margin (NIM) is going to get absolutely crushed.

![](https://blog.tipranks.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/06/image-1443.png)

Certain minor cracks in its [most recent report](https://www.tipranks.com/news/the-fly/sofi-technologies-reports-q1-eps-12c-consensus-12c-thefly-news) have given bears some ammunition. [Marketing expenses remain stubbornly high](https://www.tipranks.com/news/company-announcements/sofi-technologies-earnings-call-highlights-profitable-hypergrowth) as SoFi aggressively fights for market share, pressuring short-term margins. Additionally, conservative forward guidance occasionally triggers algorithmic selling down of the stock. There is also a lingering fear of credit deterioration, because if economic conditions weaken further, loan delinquencies could rise, forcing the bank to increase its credit loss provisions.

## Blockbuster Numbers Shatter the Bear Narrative

However, SoFi’s blockbuster numbers shatter the bear narrative, revealing that the operational reality at SoFi is decoupled from its depressed stock price. Last quarter, the digital disruptor posted a record GAAP [net revenue of $1.1 billion](https://www.tipranks.com/news/sofi-stock-plunges-10-as-stagnant-2026-forecast-and-muddy-waters-feud-eclipse-1-1-billion-in-revenue), representing an explosive 43% year-over-year jump. On an adjusted basis, net revenue reached $1.087 billion, up an equally impressive 41%. Crucially, this latest quarterly revenue growth showed clear acceleration both on a sequential quarter-over-quarter and an annual year-over-year basis.

![](https://blog.tipranks.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/06/image-1442.png)

SoFi’s profitability is improving at an even better pace. Adjusted EBITDA surged 62% year-over-year to a record $340 million. The engine driving this growth is a powerful member-acquisition flywheel. SoFi added a massive 1.1 million new members in the quarter, pushing its total [member base up 35% to 14.7 million](https://www.tipranks.com/news/sofi-earnings-sofi-stock-falters-as-investors-ignore-record-growth). Even better, cross-buying hit 43%, meaning new product adoptions came from existing users who love the app. What’s important, though, is that as we head into the Q2 print, there are few to no signs of SoFi’s momentum slowing, with management’s estimate at $1.1 billion, [implying year-over-year growth of 30%](https://www.tipranks.com/news/the-fly/sofi-technologies-sees-q2-adjusted-net-revenue-growth-of-30-thefly-news).

## Is SoFi Stock a Value Play?

The ongoing decline in SoFi stock against its ever-improving financials feels like we are watching a fintech high-flier transform into a value play. The market is pricing this business for a notable slowdown, even though we are actually seeing acceleration in top- and bottom-line growth. This disconnect has created an asymmetrical risk-reward profile where the downside is heavily insulated by compounding earnings power.

In fact, the math behind the current valuation makes a compelling case. Right now, [the stock is trading at 27.8x](https://www.tipranks.com/stocks/sofi/statistics) this year’s expected [earnings per share (EPS) of $0.60](https://www.tipranks.com/stocks/sofi/earnings). This EPS implies a massive year-over-year growth rate of 53%. In the meantime, consensus EPS estimates show a 2027 EPS of $0.79, another 33% jump. Looking further out, the year-ahead EPS is expected to grow by another 25% to reach $1.00.

Therefore, the multiple can quickly compress, and today’s valuation could prove cheap if SOFI actually beats these estimates. Paying a forward multiple in the high twenties for a company compounding its bottom line at this speed is rather rare, in my experience. If indeed the company outpaces Wall Street’s expectations, today’s entry price will look absurdly discounted.

## Is SoFi Stock a Buy, Sell, or Hold?

SOFI stock has a Hold consensus rating on Wall Street, based on six Buy, 10 Hold, and three Sell ratings. This mix reflects the ongoing concerns surrounding the stock’s sentiment. Further, [SOFI’s average price target of $20.69](https://www.tipranks.com/stocks/sofi/forecast) implies about 21% upside over the next 12 months.

![](https://blog.tipranks.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/06/SOFI-Stock-Forecast-Price-Targets.jpg)

## Conclusion

In short, I would argue that Wall Street’s short-sightedness might be creating a rare buying opportunity. SoFi is a highly profitable financial powerhouse that today looks like a beaten-down fintech stock despite management’s elite execution. Macro-related risks are likely to keep the stock under pressure in the short term. However, sentiment toward the stock should turn bullish once its upcoming reports show sustained revenue and earnings growth, especially given the stock’s current valuation.
