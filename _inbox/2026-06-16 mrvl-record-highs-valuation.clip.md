## Marvell’s AI Strength Carries Real Trade-Offs

Marvell’s AI strength carries real trade-offs. Recent quarterly results showed that sales increased by 28% year-over-year, buoyed by Marvell’s data center business, which drives 76% of total [revenue](https://www.tipranks.com/stocks/mrvl/financials). In other words, Marvell’s growth prospects are essentially a bet that the big cloud companies, or hyperscalers, such as Amazon [AMZN +3.13% ▲](https://www.tipranks.com/stocks/amzn) , Google [GOOGL +2.69% ▲](https://www.tipranks.com/stocks/googl) , and Microsoft [MSFT +2.31% ▲](https://www.tipranks.com/stocks/msft) , will continue to pour money into AI data centers. Thus, even a modest decline in capex spending by hyperscalers could hit Marvell’s revenues hard and fast. This [central risk](https://www.tipranks.com/news/marvell-mrvl-might-be-the-next-broadcom-avgo-im-not-paying-this-price-to-find-out) becomes more important as MRVL’s share price climbs further.

That risk is not theoretical. Marvell itself flags in its own filings that customers could develop their own chips or vertically integrate, reducing the need for Marvell’s products entirely. Big cloud companies typically buy from a supplier while quietly building the capability to replace them. It has not happened at scale yet, but the possibility sits inside every major customer relationship Marvell has. In addition, any meaningful macroeconomic slowdown could mean that hyperscalers need to reduce spending on some projects.

Against that backdrop, Marvell has committed [roughly $1 billion in supplier prepayments](https://www.tipranks.com/news/company-announcements/marvell-technology-signals-powerful-ai-fueled-growth) this fiscal year to lock in chip manufacturing capacity well ahead of actual orders. Meanwhile, Marvell’s share count has climbed to roughly 915 million following recent acquisitions, meaning each shareholder owns a smaller slice of the future payoff. Marvell is spending heavily and diluting today for revenue that depends entirely on its biggest customers staying the course. The gap between today’s cost and tomorrow’s reward is why I maintain a **Hold** stance on MRVL shares.

## Recent Strong Results Set a High Bar

Marvell’s latest results show that management has been delivering. The company posted [record revenue of $2.4 billion in Fiscal Q1 2027](https://www.tipranks.com/news/the-fly/marvell-reports-q1-adjusted-eps-80c-consensus-79c-thefly-news), with adjusted earnings up 29% from a year ago. High-margin AI products have also lifted Marvell’s gross margin to roughly 59%. Management raised its full-year outlook to about $11.5 billion this fiscal year and $16.5 billion next fiscal year. As a result of these impressive numbers and growth, the company will soon be added to the S&P 500 index. MRVL stock has already climbed more than 300% over the past year, reflecting much of that optimism.

However, hitting Fiscal 2028 targets means doubling revenue from the $8.2 billion Marvell posted in Fiscal 2026, across multiple new product ramps that are still in early stages today. Marvell shareholders appreciate that this is not a small ask. [For example, the Celestial AI acquisition](https://www.tipranks.com/news/mrvl-earnings-marvell-stock-rises-on-q1-results) alone does not contribute [meaningful revenue](https://www.tipranks.com/news/ratings/marvell-technology-celestial-ai-acquisition-unlocks-long-term-photonics-driven-growth-despite-near-term-eps-pressure-ratings) until the second half of Fiscal 2028, and the earnings call summary notes that several of Marvell’s largest initiatives depend on multi-year customer ramps that carry real timing risk.

Every one of those ramps needs to land on schedule, with no delays from Marvell customers, no slippage in product development, and no cooling in cloud spending. Miss any one of them, and the $16.5 billion revenue target starts to look very different. That is the bar MRVL stock is already priced for today.

## The Market Is Already Paying a Premium

Marvell stock has more than doubled since January 2026, and that run has stretched the valuation well beyond what the numbers alone justify. MRVL shares [now trade at roughly 70x forward earnings](https://www.tipranks.com/stocks/mrvl/statistics), almost double the 35x for the semiconductor industry. Even compared to the two heavyweights that are central to AI, Nvidia [NVDA +3.54% ▲](https://www.tipranks.com/stocks/nvda) and Broadcom [AVGO +3.11% ▲](https://www.tipranks.com/stocks/avgo) , MRVL trades higher. Nvidia’s [forward P/E is around 23x](https://www.tipranks.com/stocks/nvda/statistics), while Broadcom [trades around 33x](https://www.tipranks.com/stocks/avgo/statistics). The Street is paying for a future that has to unfold almost perfectly for Marvell.

The same premium valuation concerns show up in Marvell’s own history. Its price-to-sales ratio now sits near 28x; at the start of 2026, it was 8.3x, and 16.9x a year earlier. By comparison, the industry’s P/S ratio is around 18x. Investors are paying considerably more than what they have historically paid for Marvell. They are also paying more vis-à-vis the semiconductor industry. Marvell’s revenue growth may well arrive on schedule, but at today’s price, the stock leaves no room for a single misstep.

These metrics do not necessarily mean Marvell shares are destined to decline. In fact, exceptional businesses can remain expensive for extended periods. Yet from a risk-reward perspective, the upside for Marvell stock appears less compelling than it did previously. That asymmetry is precisely why I stay at **Hold**.

Meanwhile, the [average analyst price target for MRVL is $252](https://www.tipranks.com/stocks/mrvl/forecast), which implies roughly 18.41% downside from the current share price of $308.88.

## Conclusion

Marvell has successfully positioned itself as one of the key semiconductor beneficiaries of the AI era. Its custom silicon expertise, networking solutions, and strategic partnerships create a strong competitive foundation that should support growth for years to come. Nonetheless, MRVL stock’s huge rally has priced in most of that good news. Notably, MRVL’s average analyst price target of $252 is roughly 18.41% below the stock’s current price. Current holders have good reason to stay put, but new buyers may want to wait for a more attractive entry point into Marvell shares. For now, I maintain a **Hold** rating on MRVL.
