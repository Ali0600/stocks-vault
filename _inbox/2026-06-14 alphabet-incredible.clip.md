The company – one of the largest around by any metric – has been on quite a heater over the last year. In fact, the company’s share price has doubled in value during the last twelve months.

On the one hand, there’s nothing surprising about its increasing share price. Alphabet continues to break revenue records, and is now consistently clocking in more than $100 billion in quarterly sales. It has its hands in a variety of internet and technology-related fields, and is angling to be one of the biggest players in the AI industry in the years ahead.

Still, after such a strong bull run, is now the time to ease off the gas pedal? Not necessarily, according to top investor Keithen Drury.

“While it’s not a screaming deal right now, it’s also not a bad investment and will likely outperform the market moving forward if it can keep up its high, double-digit growth rates,” [says](https://www.fool.com/investing/2026/06/13/alphabet-stock-is-up-nearly-100-over-the-past-year/) the 5-star investor, who is among the top 3% of stock pros covered by TipRanks and a writer for The Motley Fool.

Gone are the days when fear over Search primacy was one of the overarching narratives, explains Drury. In fact, the opposite has occurred, with the company “masterfully” integrating AI into the Google Search engine.

While revenues for Search were up by double-digits in the last quarter, the company’s Google Cloud segment grew at an even more scorching pace of 63% year-over-year. The investor adds that Alphabet is enjoying both “massive demand” for Google Cloud’s servers and AI compute as well as generating “a ton of money” by selling its AI chips.

Though he notes that the company is trading at a premium to the market, Drury argues that it’s also growing at a faster rate. Therefore, though he wouldn’t go so far as to call GOOG undervalued, Drury also thinks it remains a good bet.
