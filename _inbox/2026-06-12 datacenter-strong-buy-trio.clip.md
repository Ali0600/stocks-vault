## Applied Digital APLD +2.97% ▲

Applied Digital is a Texas-based data center developer that is attracting attention on Wall Street. The company started out as a Bitcoin and [Ethereum](https://www.tipranks.com/cryptocurrency/eth-usd) miner but has put its focus on developing capacity for AI workloads. This week, the AI cloud infrastructure company [secured its third 15-year lease agreement](https://www.tipranks.com/news/why-is-applied-digital-stock-apld-soaring-today-june-9-2026) with the same U.S.-based hyperscaler.

The company builds facilities it calls *Applied AI Factories* and [has now contracted out 1.4 gigawatts](https://www.tipranks.com/news/why-is-applied-digital-stock-apld-soaring-today-june-9-2026) of critical IT load across its five data center campuses, with $86 billion in total contracted lease revenue if all options are exercised.

Applied Digital’s shares have jumped over 74% year-to-date. Yet, analysts see about 62% upside ahead based on an average price target of $69.11.

## GE Vernova GEV +3.74% ▲

GE Vernova is a power generation and electrification infrastructure company based in Massachusetts. The firm generates power from multiple sources, including hydro, gas, and nuclear energy. It also sells products such as transformers, switchgear, and grid systems.

GE Vernova’s shares have climbed about 44% since January. Analysts believe GEV has more room for another 33% jump in the months ahead. This is based on their average price target of $1,252.11.

## Dycom Industries DY +0.95% ▲

Dycom is a specialty contracting services company that provides engineering and construction services to the telecom and utility industries. The company also plays a key role in the data center industry: it provides electrical infrastructure, especially through its $1.95 billion acquisition of electrical contractor Power Solutions in 2025.

Dycom’s shares have climbed by roughly 40% year-to-date. Yet, analysts have an average price target of $641.11 on the stock, implying about 37% upside ahead.
