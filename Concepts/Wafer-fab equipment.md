# Wafer-fab equipment (WFE)

Wafer-fab equipment is the machinery that physically builds chips — and it's a five-company oligopoly that gates every fab on Earth. The work splits into four steps: photolithography (printing the circuit patterns), deposition (adding material layers), etch (removing material), and process control (inspection/metrology to hold yield). Five vendors control ~56–66% of the ~$90B WFE market: [[AMAT]] (deposition/etch/implant breadth), [[ASML]] (lithography — and the *sole* maker of EUV), [[LRCX]] (etch + deposition), [[KLAC]] (process control, ~52% share), and Tokyo Electron (external).

The deepest gate is ASML's EUV monopoly: no leading-edge chip ([[Foundry process nodes]]) can be printed without an ASML EUV scanner, and High-NA EUV extends that lock into the next nodes. A fab can't ramp without all four steps, so the whole group has structural pricing power and multi-year backlogs.
