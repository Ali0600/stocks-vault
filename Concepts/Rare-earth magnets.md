# Rare-earth magnets (NdFeB)

Neodymium-iron-boron (NdFeB) magnets are the strongest permanent magnets made, and they're what turn current into torque in EV motors, wind turbines, robots, and defense hardware — an F-35 uses hundreds of pounds, and guided munitions and radar depend on them. The neodymium/praseodymium (NdPr) content is where most of the value sits.

China controls roughly 85–90% of global NdFeB magnet manufacturing and is the only country that can run every processing step; Bloomberg Intelligence sees that share sliding toward ~69% by 2030 as the West scrambles to catch up. In 2025 China tightened export licenses on heavy-rare-earth (Dy/Tb) magnets and SmCo, with military-adjacent shipments rarely approved — which is exactly why a scaled domestic source like [[MP]] is treated as strategic rather than just another miner.
